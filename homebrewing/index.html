So... What's In Beer?

Water


(No surprises here)


Malted Barley


  • Soaked, heated, and dried
  • Produces enzymes, feeds the yeast during fermentation
  • Contributes to color, flavor
  • Might also use malt extract

Hops


  • Flower, member of the hemp family
  • Releases acids for bitterness, oils for flavor and aroma
  • Grown around the world

Yeast


  • Single-celled organism
  • Does the fermenting, produces carbon dioxide and alcohol

Other Stuff


  • Specialty grains, oats, wheat, etc
  • Spices: cinammon, corriander, pepper
  • Fruit: cherry, peach, orange peel
  • Coffee, chocolate... the list goes on
  • (Also love)

So... How Do You Make It?

Step 1: Gather Your Supplies

Ingredients

Pale Ale: grains, malt extract, hops, and yeast.

Tools


  • Carboy (fermenter)
  • Racking cane + tubing
  • Mesh filter + funnel
  • Pots, pans, cups
  • Bottles + caps
  • Sanitizer

Step 2: Steep the Grains

Heat a pot of water, steep for ~15 minutes.

Step 3: Add the Malt Extract

Bring to boil, stir in the malt extract until it dissolves.

Step 4: Boil the "Wort"

Boil for ~1 hour. Add hops, other ingredients throughout.

Step 5: Filter Out "Trub"

Cool pot in ice bath and transfer wort to carboy.
Filter out spent hops, etc.

Step 6: Add the Yeast

"Pitch" the yeast; add it to the carboy.

Step 7: Fermentation

Put the carboy in a dark place. Ferment for ~2 weeks (ales).

Wait for it...

Step 8: Bottling Prep

Boil sugar and water. Siphon beer from carboy to pot. Stir.

Step 9: Bottle the Beer

Siphon beer from pot into bottles. Let sit for ~2 weeks.

Wait for it...

Step 10: Enjoy!
